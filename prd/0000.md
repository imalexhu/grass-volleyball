# PRD: Adelaide-Specific Grass Volleyball Tournament System

## Problem Statement

The current app treats tournaments as generic events where anyone can register a vaguely-defined "team" (just a name + captain). There's no concept of:
- Individual players registering
- Teams as first-class entities with membership
- Enforcing the real Adelaide format (teams of 4, 8 teams per tournament)
- Player grading / ratings for fair competition

We need to reshape the data model, auth flows, and tournament lifecycle to match how Adelaide grass volleyball actually operates.

## Core Principles

1. **Adelaide first** — the UX, language, format, and enforcement are built for Adelaide grass volleyball, not generic tournaments
2. **Players first** — people register, then form teams, then compete. Players own their data
3. **Enforced structure** — 4 players per team, 8 teams per tournament. No freeform slots
4. **Fair competition** — player grading/rating system influences pool seeding

## Key Features

### Player System
- Players register individually (already works with Firebase Auth)
- Each player has a profile with: name, email, **player rating/grade** (bronze/silver/gold/platinum or numeric), join date
- Players can see their match history, team history, statistics

### Team System (First-Class)
- Teams are a top-level Firestore collection: `teams/{teamId}`
- Each team: `{id, name, captainId, members: [{userId, displayName, rating}], createdAt, tournamentIds[]}`
- Exactly 4 members per team (enforced at DB + client)
- One captain per team (can transfer captaincy)
- Players can belong to multiple teams, but only 1 team per active tournament
- Team pages: roster, match history, tournament record, stats

### Tournament Format (Adelaide-Specific)
- **Locked to 8 teams of 4** — 32 players total
- **Format**: 2 pools of 4 → Round Robin (3 matches per team in pool) → Top 2 from each pool advance to cross-pool finals
  - QF: A1 vs B4, A2 vs B3, A3 vs B2, A4 vs B1
  - SF: Winner QF1 vs Winner QF4, Winner QF2 vs Winner QF3
  - Final / Bronze match
- **Court allocation**: 2 courts standard, 3 courts for faster throughput
- **Set scoring**: Standard rally scoring to 21 (cap at 25) or to 15 (cap at 17) for finals
- **Time slots**: 30 min per pool set, 45 min per final stage
- **Adelaide venues**: dropdown of real Adelaide venues (Glenelg, Henley, Bonython Park, West Beach, Semaphore, Hahndorf) + custom

### Registration Flow (Revised)
1. Player registers (existing auth flow) → completes profile with rating
2. Player creates or joins a team (new flow)
   - Creating: names the team, becomes captain, can invite others
   - Joining: searches teams, requests to join (or captain adds directly)
3. Team (captain) browses tournaments and registers the full team
   - Registers all 4 members at once
   - Payment collects for the team (per-player or flat)
   - Team appears in tournament's registered team list
4. Tournament fills → status changes to "filled" → admin generates fixtures

### Admin/Org Dashboard Updates
- See all players and teams across the system
- Manage player ratings
- Generate fixtures when 8 teams are registered
- Manual team placement (override pool seeding based on ratings)
- Tournament completion flow (finalize standings, upload VODs)

### Existing Features to Preserve
- Live scoring with real-time Firestore subscriptions
- VOD processing pipeline (Cloud Run → YouTube)
- Stripe checkout integration
- Match events and highlights
- Team stats and match history pages

## Out of Scope (v0000)
- Mobile apps
- Automated player rating calculation (manual admin assignment for now)
- Team chat / messaging
- League/tier system across multiple tournaments
- On-court video analysis

## Success Metrics
- Individual player accounts linked to team memberships
- 8-team enforcement prevents undersized/freeform tournaments
- Team registration flow completed end-to-end
- Player grades visible and used in seeding
